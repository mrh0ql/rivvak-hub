# Gigs Strategy Playbook

> **Purpose:** How to find, price, and land one-off gigs outside of Fiverr — direct outreach, referrals, and platform diversification.

---

## Gig Sources (Priority Order)

1. **Referrals from past clients** — highest close rate, zero acquisition cost
2. **Direct outreach on LinkedIn** — slower but high-value clients
3. **Fiverr** — volume play, lower margins
4. **Reddit** (r/forhire, r/hiring, r/webdev) — occasional wins
5. **Discord communities** — dev/startup servers
6. **Twitter/X** — build in public → inbound leads
7. **Cold email** — last resort, low ROI

---

## Ideal Client Profile

**Best fit for Rivvak:**
- Solo founder or small team (2–10 people)
- Has a working product or clear MVP in mind
- Understands software; doesn't need hand-holding
- Has budget ($500+) and clear timeline
- Can make decisions quickly

**Red flags:**
- "I have an idea but no budget yet"
- Wants equity instead of payment
- Has already fired 2+ developers
- Can't clearly explain what they want
- Requires multiple approval layers

---

## Lead Qualification Script

When a lead reaches out, qualify with:

1. **What are you building?** (understand the scope)
2. **What's your timeline?** (understand urgency)
3. **What's your budget range?** (qualify early — saves both time)
4. **Have you worked with freelancers before?** (calibrate expectations)
5. **What would success look like?** (understand their definition of done)

## Pricing Framework

### By Project Type

| Project | Floor | Standard | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Landing page | $300 | $500 | $800 |
| Telegram bot | $350 | $600 | $1,000 |
| Web app (MVP) | $1,000 | $1,800 | $3,500 |
| AI automation | $500 | $900 | $1,800 |
| API integration | $400 | $700 | $1,200 |
| Full stack SaaS | $3,000 | $6,000 | $12,000+ |

### Pricing Rules
- **Never quote below floor** — if it's not worth the floor price to you, decline
- **Charge for complexity:** Every integration with a third-party API = +$100–200
- **Charge for urgency:** <48h delivery = +50%
- **Charge for ambiguity:** Unclear specs = quote higher to absorb unknowns

---

## Closing the Deal

**The fastest close:** Be specific and confident. Vague proposals lose to decisive ones.

- Quote a specific number, not a range
- Set a clear expiry: "This quote is valid for 5 days"
- Make the next step frictionless: "Reply 'yes' and I'll send the contract"
- Follow up once if no response in 48h

**Objection handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | "I understand. What's your budget? Let's see if there's a smaller scope that fits." |
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | "I'm not able to reduce the price, but I can reduce scope to fit your budget." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Of course. The quote is valid until [date]. Anything I can clarify in the meantime?" |
| "I found someone cheaper" | "That's totally fine. If it doesn't work out, I'm here." (Never match rock-bottom prices) |

## After the Gig

1. **Ask for a referral** — "If you know anyone who needs [service], I'd really appreciate the introduction"
2. **Ask for a testimonial** — short, specific, quotable
3. **Offer a retainer** — "If you have ongoing work, I offer a monthly retainer that saves you money per hour"
4. **Stay in touch** — quarterly check-in, not spammy

---

## Pipeline Tracking

Keep a simple pipeline:

| Stage | What it means |
|---|---|
| Lead | Expressed interest, not yet qualified |
| Qualified | Confirmed budget + timeline |
| Proposal Sent | Awaiting decision |
| Negotiating | Back and forth on scope/price |
| Won | Contract signed, work started |
| Lost | Didn't close (note reason) |

**Review pipeline weekly.** Anything in "Proposal Sent" for more than 5 days needs a follow-up.
