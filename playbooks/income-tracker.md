# Income Tracker Playbook

> **Purpose:** How to track, categorize, and forecast Rivvak's income across all revenue streams.

---

| Stream | Type | Avg Value | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fiverr — Web/App | One-time | $180 | ~8x/month |
| Fiverr — Bots/AI | One-time | $250 | ~4x/month |

---

## Monthly Income Log Format

Track every payment in this format:

```
| Date | Client | Platform | Service | Amount | Status | Notes |
```

**Status options:** `Invoiced` · `Paid` · `Pending` · `Cancelled`

**Example:**
```
| 2026-06-03 | Andrew / Strivve | Direct | Web App | $1,500 | Paid | Full project |
| 2026-06-05 | FiverrClient123 | Fiverr | Telegram Bot | $200 | Paid | 5-star review |
| 2026-06-10 | RetainerCo | Direct | Retainer | $700 | Paid | Month 3 |
```

| Total revenue | $2,500 | $4,500 | $8,000 |

**MRR growth goal:** Add 1 new retainer client per month until 4 active retainers ($2,800 MRR).

---

## Weekly Income Review

Every Sunday, review:

1. **Paid this week:** Total received
2. **Invoiced but unpaid:** Any overdue?
3. **Pipeline:** Leads, quotes out, proposals sent
4. **Forecast:** Expected income next 2 weeks

---

## Cash Flow Rules

- **Invoice immediately** upon project completion — never wait more than 24 hours
- **Net-7 payment terms** on direct projects (pay within 7 days)
- **50% upfront** on projects over $500 — non-negotiable
- **Chase overdue invoices** at Day 3, Day 7, Day 14 after due date

### Overdue Invoice Follow-Up Templates

**Day 3 (friendly):**
```
Hey [Name], just a gentle reminder that invoice #[X] for $[Amount] was due [Date]. Let me know if you have any questions!
```

**Day 7 (firmer):**
```
Hi [Name], following up on invoice #[X] for $[Amount], now [X] days overdue. Please process this at your earliest convenience or let me know if there's an issue.
```

**Day 14 (final):**
```
Hi [Name], this is my final follow-up regarding invoice #[X] for $[Amount]. Please process within 48 hours or I'll need to pause work until this is resolved.
```

## Tax Prep

- Set aside **25–30%** of every payment for taxes
- Keep a running total of: gross income, business expenses, net taxable
- Log all business expenses: software, hosting, tools, equipment
- Reconcile monthly — don't let it pile up until year-end

### Deductible Expenses Checklist
- [ ] Hosting (Vercel, Railway, etc.)
- [ ] Domain names
- [ ] SaaS tools (Notion, GitHub, etc.)
- [ ] AI API costs (OpenAI, Anthropic)
- [ ] Hardware/equipment
- [ ] Professional development (courses, books)
- [ ] Marketing and advertising

---

## Annual Review

At year end, calculate:
- Total gross revenue
- Revenue by stream (which streams are growing?)
- Average project value (is it trending up?)
- Client retention rate
- Hours worked vs revenue (are you pricing correctly?)

**Goal:** Increase average project value by 20% YoY without significantly increasing hours.
