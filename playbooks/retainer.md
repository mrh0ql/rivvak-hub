# Retainer Client Playbook

> **Purpose:** How to run a smooth, high-value monthly retainer relationship — from onboarding to renewal.

---

## Retainer Tiers

| Tier | Price | Hours | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $400/mo | ~5 hrs | Small tweaks, 1 new feature/month |
| Standard | $700/mo | ~10 hrs | Active product development |
| Growth | $1,200/mo | ~18 hrs | Full ongoing dev partner |

**Default:** Push Standard ($700/mo). It's the sweet spot — enough hours to deliver real value, sustainable for you.

---

## Onboarding Checklist

### Day 1 — Kickoff
- [ ] Send welcome email with contract attached
- [ ] Schedule kickoff call (30 min)
- [ ] Set up shared project doc (Notion or Google Doc)
- [ ] Get access to: repo, staging env, any APIs needed
- [ ] Log client in income tracker

### Week 1 — Setup
- [ ] Document their current stack
- [ ] List all active integrations
- [ ] Identify quick wins for first month
- [ ] Set communication cadence (weekly async update or call)

### Month 1 — Delivery
- [ ] Ship at least 1 meaningful deliverable by week 2
- [ ] Send mid-month check-in
- [ ] Prepare month-end summary

---

## Monthly Rhythm

### Week 1
- Review backlog from last month
- Confirm priorities for this month
- Start highest-priority task

### Week 2
- Mid-month async update:
  - What's been done
  - What's in progress
  - Any blockers or questions

### Week 3–4
- Complete deliverables
- QA and deploy
- Document everything new

### End of Month
- Send month-end report (see template below)
- Request renewal confirmation
- Propose next month's roadmap

**Next Month — Proposed:**

---

## Communication Rules

- **Response time:** Within 4 hours on weekdays
- **Async-first:** Most communication via message, calls only when needed
- **Weekly pulse:** Short async update every Friday — even if just "on track, no blockers"
- **No surprises:** Proactively flag anything that might delay or change scope

---

## Scope Management

Retainers cover planned work within the agreed hours. Out-of-scope requests:

1. Acknowledge the request warmly
2. Estimate hours needed
3. Offer options:
   - Include in next month's retainer hours
   - Bill as a one-time add-on
   - Upgrade to higher tier

Template:
```
Hey [Name], that's a great idea — totally doable. It's about [X hours] of work, which would go beyond this month's included hours. I can:

1. Carry it over to next month's hours
2. Quote it as a one-time add-on (~$X)
3. If this kind of work will be ongoing, we could look at bumping to the Growth tier

What works best?
```

## Renewal Process

- **Send renewal notice 10 days before end of month**
- Include: summary of value delivered, what's coming next month
- **Renewal rate target:** 90%+ — if a client is considering cancelling, have a conversation, don't just accept it

Renewal message template:
```
Hey [Name], great working with you this month. Renewal for [Next Month] is coming up — I've got [X] queued up for next month including [brief preview]. Same terms at $[Price]/mo.

Just confirm and I'll send the invoice.
```

---

## Red Flags (When to Have a Conversation)

- Client goes silent mid-month
- Scope keeps expanding without acknowledgment
- Late payments 2+ months in a row
- Disrespectful or dismissive communication
- More than 3 priority changes in a month

**Policy:** Address red flags early and directly. Most issues resolve with a single honest conversation.

---

## Offboarding (When It Ends Well)

1. Final month-end report with full summary
2. Hand off all credentials, documentation, and code
3. Offer a "maintenance only" package at reduced rate
4. Ask for a testimonial / LinkedIn recommendation
5. Stay in touch — former clients are future referral sources
